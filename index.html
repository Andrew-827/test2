DIY Diagnostics Concussion

Are you experiencing a concussion?

This test will help determine whether you have a concussion. Although it does not replace seeing a physician, it can assess signs on brain injury. If you are experiencing or have experienced losing consciousness, inability to awaken, seizures, numbness, or confusion at any point after your injury, call 911 or go to your closest emergency room immediately.


Instructions:

  • There will be a higher accuracy if patient is assessed by another individual.
  • If performing a peer-assessment, assist the patient and log the answers appropriately.
  • If performing a self-assessment, answer truthfully to the best of your ability.

    Symptom Check

    1. Are you experiencing a headache or pressure in your head?



    2. Any nausea or vomiting since the incident?



    3. Do you feel dizzy or have blurred vision?



    4. Are you bothered by or feel sensitive to noise or light?



    5. Are you just not "feeling right" or "feeling down"?



    6. Are you experiencing numbness or weakness in any area of your body?



    7. Are you experiencing ringing in your ears?

    Observed Symptoms

    8. Was there any loss of consciousness (even briefly)?



    9. Did the patient have a seizure or convulsions? (Shaking or twitching)



    10. Does the patient answer questions slowly?



    11. Does the patient have slurred speech or speech difficulty?



    12. Does the patient appear dazed or stunned?



    13. Does the patient move clumsily?



    14. Does the patient show mood, behavior, or personality changes?
